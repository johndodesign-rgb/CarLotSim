Welcome to the lot
You're the dealer.
They want the car.
Each round a buyer walks onto your lot. Your job is simple: set the right asking price for the car they're looking at.
The catch: you never see the true market value up front. You have to read the car's condition, mileage, and the buyer's signals — then make your call.
Price too high and they walk. Price too low and you leave money on the table. The closer you get to true value, the better your score.
